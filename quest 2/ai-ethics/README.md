# AI Ethics & Responsible AI Use

This document summarizes key lessons and exercises completed so far on AI ethics, bias, and learning amplification.

# Detecting & Mitigating Harmful Outputs

AI can produce biased or misleading outputs, so reviewing and revising prompts is essential. Human review is needed to assess factual errors, potential harm, and assumptions.  

# Understanding AI Bias

AI reflects historical and societal biases present in training data. These bias can impact sectors like hiring, healthcare, and criminal justice. To ensure this doesn't happen we must check our prompt design, balance data, choose the right, and continuous monitoring.

# Designing Ethical Prompts

Poor prompts can reinforce stereotypes or promote unethical behavior, by rewriting prompts we can remove assumptions and focus on fairness to produce safer outputs. Testing and validating prompts also ensure accuracy and ethical compliance.

# AI as Learning Amplifier

Attempting problems independently before using AI can strengthen understanding. AI can also help identify rear problems and provide solutions without replacing thinking. Studying routing protocols and designing smart-city networks showed the value of system-level reasoning.
